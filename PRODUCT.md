# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

NJ homeowners and commercial / industrial facility managers searching for a licensed local electrician for projects (panel upgrades, generators, lighting, hot tub wiring, new construction) or emergency response (sparking outlets, dead panels, burning smells). They land here from Google, a referral, or the existing WordPress site they're replacing. Most are decision-ready or close to it: they're checking credentials, service area, and getting a quote — not browsing for inspiration.

## Product Purpose

Lead generation site for Mike DiNinno Electric, LLC (sole proprietor, veteran-owned, est. 2013, NJ Lic #17639). Success = phone calls to 973.787.7122 and Formspree submissions on the contact page. Secondary success = SCA (Service Contract Agreement) inquiries from commercial property owners. The site has to *feel* like Mike — owner-operator, hands-on, no sales reps in between — while still reading as a premium professional operation, not a one-truck shop.

## Brand Personality

Cinematic. Electric. Veteran-owned. The Concept 3 "Electric Storm" aesthetic: deep dark backgrounds, cyan/purple lightning, plasma orbs, animated grids — "if Apple did electrical contracting." Three-word voice: **Confident · Hands-on · Dramatic.** Mike on every job. 30 years in the field, not the office. Premium without being precious. The lightning is the brand.

## Anti-references

- Generic blue-and-white electrician sites with clipart of a smiling guy in a polo
- The 2010s "trades site": cluttered, three CTAs above the fold, autoplaying carousel
- Yelp / Angi-style category-page filler design
- Generic SaaS dashboards (this is a service business, not a product)
- Suburban-realtor stock photography
- Em dashes in copy (yes really)
- Numbered section eyebrows (01 · About / 02 · Process) as default scaffolding

## Design Principles

1. **Show the work, not the company.** Every visual element earns its place by selling a job, not by decorating. The lightning grid + plasma orb suggest power and precision, not "we have a website."
2. **The lightning is the brand voice.** Cyan/purple electric motion is the visual signature — anywhere the site needs personality, that's where it comes from. No fallback to bland.
3. **Mike-on-every-job, in every section.** Copy is first-person operator, not corporate "we provide solutions". Bio facts (Navy ETS, DePaul '81, four daughters, fishes) earn trust without resume-speak.
4. **Convert on the page, every page.** Phone in the topbar (visible site-wide), red emergency bar after every hero, CTA buttons that say what happens ("Request a Free Estimate" not "Get Started"), 973.787.7122 four+ times per page minimum.
5. **Premium dark without sacrificing readability.** Cinematic motion behind, but body copy hits 4.5:1+ contrast and 16px minimum. The wow doesn't fight the read.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG AA target across the board (body text ≥4.5:1 contrast; we're closer to AAA with white on near-black at ~21:1)
- `prefers-reduced-motion: reduce` honored — kills lightning bolt animations, plasma rotation, ring expansion, and orb pulse
- Global `:focus-visible` keyboard focus ring (2px cyan) on every link, button, input
- Skip-link helper present in CSS for screen readers
- All images have descriptive `alt` text; decorative images use `alt=""`
- Tap targets ≥44×44px (mobile menu items, CTAs, topbar phone)
- Form labels are explicit on the contact form; placeholders are hints, not labels
- Mobile menu is a full-screen overlay with one entry point and one exit (no nested gestures)
