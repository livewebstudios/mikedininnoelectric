## BLOG POST 2 — How to Choose the Right Electrician: What to Ask Before Hiring
**Slug:** blog/how-to-choose-an-electrician/index.html
**Date:** April 5, 2025 | **Category:** Hiring & Planning Advice

Electrical work isn't something you want to leave to chance. Whether you're rewiring a room, adding circuits for a renovation, or handling an emergency repair — the electrician you choose matters. Here's what to ask before you hire.

**1. Are You Licensed and Insured?**
Always verify. A licensed NJ electrical contractor has passed the required exams and carries liability insurance. This protects you, your property, and ensures all work meets local code.

**2. How Long Have You Been in Business?**
Experience matters in the electrical trade. Mike DiNinno brings 30+ years of hands-on knowledge across residential, commercial, and industrial work — not years of office time, but years in the field.

**3. Do You Guarantee Your Work in Writing?**
Any professional contractor will stand behind their work. Ask for the guarantee in writing before any work begins.

**4. Can You Provide References?**
Follow up on references — don't just collect the list. Ask whether work was done on time, on budget, and to their satisfaction. Ask if they would hire again.

**5. Is the Estimate Clear and Itemized?**
Before you commit, you should understand exactly what you're paying for: labor, materials, permits, and any potential extras. No surprises.

Mike DiNinno Electric is always happy to answer these questions — and more. We've built our reputation on transparency, craftsmanship, and treating your home the way we'd treat our own.

## BLOG POST 3 — 5 Signs It's Time to Upgrade Your Electrical Panel
**Slug:** blog/5-signs-upgrade-electrical-panel/index.html
**Date:** April 5, 2025 | **Category:** Electrical Safety

Your electrical panel is the heart of your home's power system. If it's outdated, undersized, or overloaded, you may be heading toward a serious safety hazard — or already there. Here are five signs that it's time to call Mike DiNinno Electric.

**1. Your Panel Is Over 25 Years Old**
Older panels weren't built for today's power demands. If your home was built before the 1990s and has never had a panel upgrade, it's time for an assessment.

**2. You Still Have a Fuse Box**
Fuse boxes are outdated and no longer meet modern safety codes. A fuse box is a clear signal that a breaker panel upgrade is overdue.

**3. You've Added High-Draw Appliances**
Central AC, EV chargers, electric ranges, and home office equipment can push an older panel past its capacity. If you've added any of these without upgrading the panel, you're running a risk.

**4. Breakers That Trip Regularly**
Frequent breaker trips mean your circuit is overloaded or your panel is struggling to handle your home's load. This doesn't fix itself.

**5. Flickering or Dimming Lights**
Especially when major appliances kick on, this is your system telling you it's straining. It's a symptom, not the problem — and the problem lives in your panel.

A panel upgrade improves safety, increases capacity, and can boost home resale value. If you're experiencing any of these issues, call Mike DiNinno Electric at 973.787.7122 — we'll evaluate your system and recommend the right solution.
